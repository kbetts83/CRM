I was that kid in the 7th grade who learned HTML for fun. I was also that kid who would ignore assignments and work on his own projects in 11th grade Computer Science class.

I’ve always been a deeply curious person and am excited to jump into projects where I can learn about the nuts and bolts of different subjects. That curiosity became electric when I was in an introductory Computer Science course in High School and ran that first “Hello World” program. I remember being so engaged and excited learning how to create my own programs.

It wasn’t until August of 2019 that I really started to focus and dedicate myself to studying software development and commit to becoming a Jr. Software Developer. On August 12th I made an excel spreadsheet to track the daily hours I put into learning how to code. I didn’t always hit my two hour a day goal (1.66 hours per day isn’t bad when there’s a newborn baby in your life), but I always found even a few minutes to learn even if it was just sitting down to read about data structures and algorithms.

Right now I’m focusing on Python and MySQL to build my own apps and contribute to open source projects and gaining Codemy Google Certifications.
